# R---UK-election-2019

🇬🇧 UK General Election 2019: Data Analysis
Uncovering Electoral Dynamics through Statistical Modeling in R
🧐 The Research Objective

The goal of this project is to perform a deep dive into the 2019 UK General Election results. By analyzing data at the constituency level, we aim to understand the geographic distribution of party support, the correlation between voter turnout and party success, and the competitive relationship between the UK’s major political forces.
🛠️ The Analytical Stack

    Language: R

    Environment: RStudio / R Markdown

    Key Libraries: * tidyverse (dplyr, ggplot2) for data manipulation and high-quality plotting.

        knitr for report generation.

    Data Source: Official 2019 UK General Election constituency-level results.

📂 Repository Structure

    RMarkdown1_SIMATOVIC_LUCAS.Rmd: The primary analysis script containing data cleaning, statistical tests, and visualization code.

    RMarkdown1_SIMATOVIC_LUCAS.pdf: The final rendered report including all code, outputs, and interpretations.

    Consignes.pdf: The original assignment guidelines (Aix-Marseille School of Economics).

    images/: (Referenced in the report) Visualizations of vote distributions, correlations, and party performance.

📈 Key Insights & Visualizations
1. Vote Share Distribution

We analyzed how vote shares were distributed across all 650 constituencies. The density plots reveal the different "safe seat" profiles for the Labour and Conservative parties.
2. Turnout Correlation

The analysis investigates whether higher voter turnout benefited specific parties. By plotting turnout against vote shares, we can identify demographic mobilization trends.
3. The Labour-Conservative Competitive Landscape

A key part of the study explores the correlation between the two major parties. We visualize how the success of one often comes at the direct expense of the other in "swing" constituencies.
4. Small Party Dynamics

The project also examines the performance of the Green Party and the Brexit Party, identifying the specific types of constituencies where these parties managed to capture significant vote shares.
🚀 How to Use

    Clone the repository.

    Open the project: Launch RMarkdown1_SIMATOVIC_LUCAS.Rmd in RStudio.

    Install dependencies: Ensure tidyverse and ggplot2 are installed.

    Knit the file: Click "Knit" to reproduce the full PDF analysis and all associated plots.
